About Me

My journey into medicine was driven by a singular passion: to alleviate suffering through surgical intervention. As a Senior Consultant Surgeon at the B. P. Koirala Memorial Cancer Hospital (BPKMCH), I have witnessed firsthand the physical and emotional toll that a cancer diagnosis takes on patients and their families. This understanding shapes my approach to every case. I believe that a surgeon's role extends far beyond the operating theatre; it involves being a pillar of support, a source of clear information, and a partner in the patient's journey toward recovery.

While my primary focus is on the surgical eradication of tumors, I am equally passionate about reconstructive techniques that preserve dignity and quality of life. Whether it is constructing a new bladder after a cystectomy or performing nerve-sparing procedures, my goal is to ensure that life after cancer is as full and normal as possible. My training in China and the United States has equipped me with diverse perspectives, allowing me to adapt best practices to our local context in Nepal.

Outside of my professional life, I find balance in the serenity of nature. Trekking in the Himalayas is not just a hobby but a spiritual reset that keeps me grounded. I am also an avid badminton player, which helps maintain the physical stamina required for long surgical procedures. Above all, I cherish the moments spent with my friends and family, who provide the unwavering support that allows me to serve my patients effectively.

Clinical Expertise

Uro-Oncology

Uro-oncology is a highly specialized field dealing with cancers of the urinary tract and male reproductive system. My expertise covers the comprehensive management of Bladder Cancer, Prostate Cancer, Kidney Cancer, and Penile Cancer. These conditions often require intricate surgical solutions. For instance, in muscle-invasive bladder cancer, simply removing the tumor is not enough; we must often perform a Radical Cystectomy. I specialize in creating Orthotropic Neobladders (studer pouches), where a new bladder is constructed from the patient's own intestine, allowing for natural voiding and significantly improved quality of life compared to external bags.

General Oncology

Beyond urology, my surgical practice encompasses a broad spectrum of General Surgical Oncology. I have significant experience in treating Breast Cancer, emphasizing breast-conserving surgery and Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy to minimize complications like lymphedema. I also treat Rectal Cancer and soft tissue sarcomas. My approach involves a multidisciplinary team effort, working closely with medical oncologists, radiation oncologists, and pathologists to formulate a personalized treatment plan for every patient, ensuring that surgery is timed perfectly within the broader scope of therapy.

Advanced Procedures

Surgery is an art that requires constant refinement. I perform a high volume of complex procedures including Radical Prostatectomy for prostate cancer, Nephrectomy (partial and radical) for kidney tumors, and surgeries for benign conditions like BPH (Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia) using TURP (Transurethral Resection of the Prostate). I am also adept at managing stone diseases and urinary tract infections that often complicate oncological cases. My training allows me to handle intra-operative complications with confidence, ensuring patient safety remains the paramount priority during these major interventions.

Professional Biography

My academic journey began with a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) from Shanghai Medical University, where I laid the foundation of my medical knowledge. Recognizing the growing need for specialized cancer care, I pursued a Master of Surgery (M.S.) in Surgical Oncology from Fudan University, followed by an M.Ch in Urology from Kathmandu University. These degrees provided me with the rigorous academic background necessary to tackle complex oncological cases.

However, medicine is a field of lifelong learning. I sought further specialization through international fellowships. I was awarded the Saudavar Fellowship in Surgical Oncology at the world-renowned Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (USA) and the UICC ICRETT Fellowship at H. Lee Moffitt Cancer Center (USA). These experiences exposed me to cutting-edge techniques and the highest standards of patient care, which I have since implemented in my practice in Nepal.

Leadership has been a core component of my career. I have had the honor of serving as the Deputy Director (2018–2020) and subsequently the Chairman of the Board of Directors (2020–2023) at the B. P. Koirala Memorial Cancer Hospital. In these roles, I navigated the challenges of hospital administration, policy-making, and strategic planning, always with the goal of improving patient access and quality of care. I have also chaired scientific committees for national conferences, contributing to the academic growth of the oncology community in Nepal.

My commitment to research remains strong. I have authored over 30 peer-reviewed publications in national and international journals, focusing on penile cancer, bladder cancer, and breast cancer epidemiology in Nepal. I believe that documenting our local data is crucial for understanding disease patterns and improving outcomes for our specific population.

Social Responsibility & Philosophy

I firmly believe that healthcare is a fundamental human right, not a privilege. In a developing nation like Nepal, geographic and economic barriers often prevent patients from seeking timely care. To combat this, I am actively involved in organizing free medical camps and cancer screening programs in remote and underserved areas. These camps are vital for the early detection of cancer, which significantly improves survival rates.

My advocacy extends to the policy level, where I advise on government health initiatives to strengthen the national cancer control strategy. Whether it is advocating for better tobacco control laws or securing funding for advanced hospital equipment, my mission is to elevate the standard of healthcare for all Nepalese citizens. I also take great pride in mentoring the next generation of surgeons, ensuring that the legacy of skilled, compassionate care continues long into the future.
